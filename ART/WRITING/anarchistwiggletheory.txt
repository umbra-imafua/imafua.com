 Anarchist wiggle theory
Anarchist wiggle theory is an refinement of accelerationism within the goals of an anarchist framework. It supposes that in order to move toward a truly non-hierarchical and truly egalitarian society we must make trade offs to gain ground in each aspect ( freedom or equity ).

For example, in order to distribute resources to accurately suit peoples needs those people will have to have some of their personal information beurocraticaly collected and scrutinised therefore losing some of the liberty of anonymity.

On the other hand in order to give people the liberty of being able to assert what material help they believe is right for them and their circumstance; that is, giving those in need money instead of food, clothes or housing an equity imbalance will surely appear, as those who are privileged with financial education or good mental health will tend to make better financial decisions, which will lead to more likelihood of them meeting their material needs.

With wiggle theory trade offs are usually considered to be worth it if they appear to move one vector more steps forward than they move the other backwards.

Making a new novel problem alongside a impact-full solution also usually has the effect of that minor problem being solved faster, as more resources are put towards a problem we can see than a theoretical one.
It is assumed that with the improvement of science, culture, education and morality that these minor issues will one day be solved, therefore preparing us to wiggle the progress in the issue along another axis.

Obviously real world isues have more factors than just equity or freedom, this language is simply supposed to be a maluable simplification.
