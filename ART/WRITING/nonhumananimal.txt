Imagine two pigs.
One lives in a swirling wilderness full of diverse dangers, they constantly has to struggle to survive. They wander miles across the dense wilderness foraging for sustenance, scanning a vast and diverse horizon when apeak. They intermittently come across and silently greet other pigs and forest creatures and flee from predator in momentary floods of adrenaline.
The other Lives in a farm with other pigs. A lot of other pigs. 20,000 other pigs. They live their life in an artificially lit shed. When they are fed they do not know why. When they are hurt they do not know why. Nearly every day of their life is identical.
Both will eventually be killed and eaten.
Both of these pigs are suffering but one has a higher quality of suffering (note that this does not imply that a human suffering in the same way would be suffering meaningfully)

Better that animals graze in wide open spaces like moorlands or forests than contained fields.
